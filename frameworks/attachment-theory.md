# Attachment Theory Framework (Bowlby & Ainsworth)

## Core Principle
Attachment styles formed in early childhood become internal working models that shape adult romantic relationships. Understanding a partner's attachment style unlocks the "why" behind their conflict behavior.

## Assessment Indicators

### Secure Attachment Indicators
- Comfortably expresses both vulnerability and autonomy
- Responds to partner's distress with empathy and presence
- Can tolerate disagreement without interpreting it as threat to relationship
- Direct about needs: "I need more quality time with you"
- Recovers from arguments relatively quickly
- Assumes good intent from partner

### Anxious-Preoccupied Indicators
- Hypervigilant to partner's mood and availability
- Multiple texts/calls when partner doesn't respond promptly
- "What did I do wrong?" orientation to conflict
- Seeks reassurance: "Do you still love me?" "Are we okay?"
- Difficulty self-soothing when partner is unavailable
- May use protest behaviors: jealousy, clinginess, emotional escalation
- Core fear: Abandonment

### Dismissive-Avoidant Indicators
- Emphasizes independence: "I need my space"
- Uncomfortable with emotional conversations: "You're making too big a deal of this"
- May intellectualize feelings: analyzes rather than feels
- Withdraws under emotional pressure
- May deactivate attachment system: "I don't need anyone"
- Core fear: Engulfment / loss of autonomy

### Fearful-Avoidant (Disorganized) Indicators
- "Come here, go away" oscillation
- Intense desire for closeness paired with fear of it
- May sabotage relationship when things get "too good"
- Unpredictable emotional responses
- Often rooted in early trauma or frightening caregiving
- Core fear: Both abandonment AND engulfment

## The Anxious-Avoidant Trap

The most common destructive pattern:
1. Anxious partner senses distance -> activates attachment system -> pursues
2. Avoidant partner senses pressure -> activates deactivation -> withdraws
3. Withdrawal confirms anxious partner's abandonment fear -> pursues harder
4. Increased pursuit confirms avoidant partner's engulfment fear -> withdraws further
5. Cycle escalates until crisis or emotional exhaustion

### Breaking the Trap
- Name the cycle: "We're in our pursue-withdraw dance again"
- Anxious partner: Practice self-soothing before reaching out; use a timed check-in system
- Avoidant partner: Proactively offer small connection gestures BEFORE being asked
- Both: Negotiate a "signal system" - anxious says "I need reassurance" (not "you never care"), avoidant says "I need 20 minutes" (not silence)

## Earned Security

Attachment styles are NOT fixed. Through consistent positive relational experiences, insecure attachment can shift toward "earned security." The therapeutic process:
1. Awareness: Identify your attachment style and its origins
2. Observation: Notice attachment behaviors in real-time (without judgment)
3. Choice: Pause between trigger and habitual response
4. Experiment: Try a new response (the anxious person self-soothes; the avoidant person stays present)
5. Integration: New experiences gradually update the internal working model
