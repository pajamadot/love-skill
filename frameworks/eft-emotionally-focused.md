# Emotionally Focused Therapy (EFT) for Couples - Sue Johnson

## Core Principle
Distress in relationships is fundamentally about emotional disconnection. The problem is never the content of the argument - it's the loss of secure emotional bond. EFT helps couples identify and transform the negative interaction cycles that maintain insecure attachment.

## The Three Stages of EFT

### Stage 1: De-Escalation (Cycles 1-4)

**Step 1: Identify the Negative Cycle**
Map the "dance" - the repeating pattern of interaction.

Common cycles:
- **Pursue-Withdraw**: "The more I reach for you, the more you pull away. The more you pull away, the more desperately I reach."
- **Attack-Attack**: Both partners escalate, competing for dominance, neither feeling heard.
- **Withdraw-Withdraw**: Both shut down, creating emotional desert. Relationship dies of neglect.

**Step 2: Identify the Attachment Emotions Beneath the Cycle**
Surface emotions (anger, frustration) protect deeper attachment emotions:

| Surface Emotion | Deeper Attachment Emotion |
|---|---|
| Anger / Criticism | Fear of abandonment, hurt from feeling unseen |
| Contempt / Sarcasm | Helplessness, shame about needing partner |
| Withdrawal / Shutdown | Fear of inadequacy, terror of partner's rejection |
| Control / Demands | Panic about losing connection, feeling unworthy |

**Step 3: Access the Unacknowledged Emotions**
Help each partner move from reactive position to vulnerable primary emotion:

Reactive: "You never listen to me!" (anger)
Primary: "When I can't reach you, I feel terrified that I don't matter to you." (fear + attachment need)

**Step 4: Reframe the Problem as the Cycle**
"The enemy is not your partner - it's the cycle you get stuck in. You are BOTH victims of this pattern."

### Stage 2: Restructuring the Bond (Cycles 5-7)

**Step 5: Access and Express Deeper Needs and Fears**
The withdrawer risks engagement:
"I shut down because I'm so afraid of failing you. I feel like nothing I do is enough, and that paralyzes me."

**Step 6: Promote Acceptance of Partner's New Expression**
Help the other partner receive vulnerability without dismissing or rescuing:
"When you tell me you're afraid of failing me... I had no idea. I thought you just didn't care."

**Step 7: Facilitate Bonding Events ("Hold Me Tight" Conversations)**
Both partners express needs from a place of vulnerability:
- "I need to know I matter to you"
- "I need to know you won't leave even when things are hard"
- "Can you hold me when I'm scared?"

These bonding events are the turning point - they create new emotional experiences that reshape the attachment bond.

### Stage 3: Consolidation (Cycles 8-9)

**Step 8: New Solutions Emerge Naturally**
When the emotional bond is secure, practical problems that seemed impossible become solvable. Couples co-create solutions from a position of safety.

**Step 9: Consolidate New Positions**
The new interaction pattern becomes the couple's default. They can now:
- Recognize when they're falling into the old cycle
- Name it ("We're doing our thing again")
- Choose to be vulnerable instead of reactive
- Repair more quickly after ruptures

## Key EFT Interventions

### Reflection
Mirror the client's experience with emotional attunement:
"So when they come home and go straight to their phone, something happens inside you... a sinking feeling?"

### Validation
Normalize the emotional response in attachment terms:
"Of course you withdraw. When you've been told your whole life that your feelings are too much, shutting down is the safest thing you know."

### Evocative Responding
Heighten emotional experience to access deeper layers:
"What happens inside when you hear your partner say those words? Stay with that feeling for a moment..."

### Heightening
Amplify key attachment-relevant emotions:
"That's so important - you said 'I feel invisible.' Can you say that directly to your partner? 'When you turn away, I feel invisible.'"

### Empathic Conjecture
Offer a tentative guess about unspoken emotion:
"I wonder if underneath all that anger, there might be a small part of you that's just really, really sad... and maybe scared that you've lost something precious?"

### Enactment
Guide partners to turn toward each other and express directly:
"Can you turn to them right now and tell them what you just told me? About the fear?"

## The "Hold Me Tight" Conversation Structure

Seven conversations that build secure attachment:

1. **Recognizing the Demon Dialogues** - Name the negative cycle
2. **Finding the Raw Spots** - Identify attachment wounds that get triggered
3. **Revisiting a Rocky Moment** - Replay a fight through the EFT lens
4. **Hold Me Tight** - The core bonding conversation (vulnerability + responsiveness)
5. **Forgiving Injuries** - Process attachment injuries
6. **Bonding Through Sex and Touch** - Integrate physical and emotional intimacy
7. **Keeping Your Love Alive** - Maintain and protect the bond

## A.R.E. - The Core Question

All attachment distress reduces to one question: **A.R.E. you there for me?**

- **A**ccessible: Can I reach you? Will you pay attention to me?
- **R**esponsive: Will you respond to my emotional needs? Will you care?
- **E**ngaged: Will you value me and stay close? Am I special to you?

When the answer to A.R.E. is yes, the relationship feels safe.
When the answer is uncertain, attachment alarm activates and the negative cycle begins.

## Integration with Other Frameworks

| Combined With | Integration Point |
|---|---|
| Attachment Theory | EFT IS attachment theory applied. Use attachment styles to predict cycle positions. |
| Gottman | EFT's "bonding events" = Gottman's "turning toward." EFT adds emotional depth. |
| NVC | NVC's "needs" map onto EFT's "attachment needs." NVC provides the language structure. |
| Psychoanalytic | EFT's "raw spots" = psychoanalytic "wounds." EFT makes unconscious explicit in-session. |
| Polyvagal | EFT's de-escalation aligns with ventral vagal activation. Safety first. |
