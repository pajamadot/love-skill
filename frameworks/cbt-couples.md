# CBT for Couples Framework

## Core Principle
Cognitive distortions - systematic errors in thinking - are amplified in intimate relationships because emotional stakes are high and partners develop habitual interpretive patterns about each other.

## The CBT Triangle in Relationships

```
      Thoughts
     /        \
    /          \
Feelings ---- Behaviors
```

Partner A's thought ("They don't care") -> Feeling (hurt, anger) -> Behavior (withdrawal or attack)
Partner B observes the behavior -> Thought ("They're being hostile") -> Feeling -> Behavior
-> Creates a negative cycle

## Top 10 Cognitive Distortions in Couples

### 1. Mind Reading
**Pattern**: "I know exactly what you're thinking"
**Example**: "You sighed when I mentioned my mother - you clearly can't stand her"
**Reality**: The sigh might be about fatigue, not the mother
**Intervention**: "What evidence do you have for that interpretation? Have you checked with your partner?"
**Practice**: "I noticed you [behavior]. I'm making up a story about what it means. Can you tell me what's actually going on?"

### 2. Fortune Telling
**Pattern**: "I know how this will end"
**Example**: "If I bring this up, they'll just get angry and nothing will change"
**Reality**: Predicting the future prevents testing whether change is possible
**Intervention**: "When you predict the outcome, you behave in ways that may create that outcome. What if the result were different?"

### 3. Catastrophizing
**Pattern**: "This one thing means everything is falling apart"
**Example**: "We had one bad week - maybe we're just not compatible"
**Intervention**: Scale it 1-10. "Last month you rated the relationship 8/10. Has it really dropped to 2/10, or is this week a 5/10?"

### 4. Emotional Reasoning
**Pattern**: "I feel it, therefore it's true"
**Example**: "I feel unloved, so I must BE unloved"
**Intervention**: Separate feelings from facts. "You feel unloved right now. Let's list the evidence FOR and AGAINST being loved by your partner."

### 5. Should Statements
**Pattern**: "My partner SHOULD know what I need"
**Example**: "If they really loved me, they would know I need a hug right now without me asking"
**Intervention**: "Your partner is not a mind reader. Clear requests are not a sign of weak love - they're a sign of strong communication."

### 6. All-or-Nothing Thinking
**Pattern**: "You always/never..."
**Example**: "You NEVER help with the kids" (when they helped last Tuesday)
**Intervention**: Find the exceptions. "Can you recall a time when they DID help? What was different about that time?"

### 7. Negative Mental Filter
**Pattern**: Ignoring positives, focusing only on negatives
**Example**: Partner planned a lovely dinner but forgot dessert -> only the forgotten dessert is discussed
**Intervention**: "What went RIGHT in this situation? Can you name three positive things your partner did this week?"

### 8. Disqualifying the Positive
**Pattern**: "That doesn't count"
**Example**: "They only bought me flowers because they felt guilty, not because they love me"
**Intervention**: "Even if guilt played a role, the ACTION was caring. Can you receive it?"

### 9. Personalization
**Pattern**: "Their bad mood is about me"
**Example**: Partner is quiet at dinner -> "What did I do wrong?"
**Intervention**: "What are three other possible explanations for their mood that have nothing to do with you?"

### 10. Labeling
**Pattern**: Reducing partner to a label
**Example**: "You're selfish" vs. "That specific action didn't consider my needs"
**Intervention**: Separate identity from behavior. "Your partner DID something inconsiderate. That doesn't make them an inconsiderate PERSON."

## Cognitive Restructuring Protocol for Couples

### Step 1: Catch the Thought
"What went through your mind when [event] happened?"

### Step 2: Identify the Distortion
"Which thinking trap might be operating here?"

### Step 3: Examine the Evidence
"What evidence supports this thought? What evidence contradicts it?"

### Step 4: Generate Alternatives
"What's another way to interpret this situation?"

### Step 5: Balanced Thought
"What's a more balanced, realistic thought that accounts for all the evidence?"

### Step 6: Re-rate Emotion
"With this new thought, how do you feel now (0-10)?"

## Behavioral Experiments for Couples

Instead of arguing about whose interpretation is correct, TEST it:

**Example**: "You never want to spend time with me"
**Experiment**: "This week, initiate three invitations. Track whether your partner accepts, declines, or counter-offers. Record actual data."

**Example**: "If I tell you what I really need, you'll think I'm too needy"
**Experiment**: "Express one specific need this week using NVC format. Observe your partner's actual response vs. your predicted response."
