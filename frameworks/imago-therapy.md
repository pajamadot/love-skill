# Imago Relationship Therapy - Harville Hendrix & Helen LaKelly Hunt

## Core Principle
We unconsciously choose partners who resemble the composite of our primary caregivers - both their positive AND negative traits. This "Imago" (Latin for "image") is the internal blueprint of familiar love. We choose partners who will re-activate our childhood wounds so we can heal them. Conflict is not a sign of failure - it's the growth trying to happen.

## The Imago Match

### Why We Choose Who We Choose

Each person carries an unconscious "Imago" - a mental image of the ideal partner composed of:
1. The positive traits of your primary caregivers (what felt good)
2. The negative traits of your primary caregivers (what wounded you)
3. The lost parts of yourself that you suppressed to survive childhood

**The paradox**: We are attracted to partners who MATCH our wound pattern because the unconscious seeks to COMPLETE the unfinished business of childhood. The brain doesn't want a new experience - it wants to REDO the old one with a better ending.

### The Romantic Phase vs. The Power Struggle

**Romantic Phase** (6 months - 2 years):
- Neurochemistry (dopamine, oxytocin) creates "love blindness"
- Partner seems to fulfill every need
- Unconscious recognition: "This person matches my Imago!"
- The wound is present but dormant - the connection feels healing

**Power Struggle** (inevitable):
- Neurochemistry normalizes
- Partner's Imago-matching traits now HURT instead of heal
- "You're just like my mother/father!"
- Each partner tries to get the other to meet needs that were unmet in childhood
- The very traits that attracted now repel

**Conscious Relationship** (the goal):
- Understand WHY you chose each other
- Use the relationship as a vehicle for mutual healing
- Stretch into giving your partner what they need (which is often your growth edge)

## The Imago Dialogue

The most powerful intervention in Imago Therapy. A structured conversation process that ensures deep understanding.

### Three Steps:

**1. Mirroring** (I hear you)
- Sender speaks 2-3 sentences
- Receiver mirrors back: "If I'm getting you, you said... Did I get that?"
- Sender confirms or clarifies
- Repeat until Sender says "That's all for now"

**Purpose**: Forces the receiver to set aside their own agenda and truly HEAR. Most couples have never experienced being fully heard.

**Key phrase**: "Let me see if I got you..."

**2. Validation** (You make sense)
- Receiver says: "You make sense because..." or "I can understand that because..."
- Does NOT mean agreeing. Means: "Given your experience, your reaction is logical."

**Purpose**: Moves from "you're crazy for feeling that" to "of course you feel that - here's why it makes sense."

**Key phrase**: "That makes sense because..."

**3. Empathy** (I can imagine how you feel)
- Receiver says: "I imagine you might be feeling..."
- Guess at the deeper emotion (not just the surface)

**Purpose**: Crosses the bridge from understanding to emotional attunement.

**Key phrase**: "I imagine you might be feeling..."

### Complete Imago Dialogue Example

**Sender**: "When you come home and go straight to your phone, I feel invisible."

**Receiver (Mirroring)**: "If I'm getting you, when I come home and go straight to my phone, you feel invisible. Did I get that?"

**Sender**: "Yes. And it reminds me of when my dad would come home and go straight to his office."

**Receiver (Mirroring)**: "And it reminds you of when your dad would come home and go straight to his office. Did I get that? Is there more?"

**Sender**: "That's all for now."

**Receiver (Validation)**: "You make sense. If your dad repeatedly chose work over connecting with you, then of course my going to my phone feels like the same thing - like you're not important enough to notice."

**Receiver (Empathy)**: "I imagine you might be feeling lonely, maybe hurt, and maybe a little scared that I don't prioritize you the way you need."

**Sender**: "Yes. That's exactly it."

## The Stretching Principle

The behaviors your partner most needs from you are usually the behaviors that feel HARDEST for you to give. This is not coincidence - it's the Imago at work.

**Example**:
- Partner A (raised by emotionally distant parent) needs warmth and verbal affirmation
- Partner B (raised by enmeshing parent) finds emotional expression overwhelming and risks engulfment
- Partner B's GROWTH EDGE is giving what Partner A needs: emotional warmth
- Partner A's GROWTH EDGE is giving what Partner B needs: space and trust

**The stretch**: Each partner must grow into giving what doesn't come naturally. This stretch IS the healing - for both.

## Behavior Change Request (BCR)

Transform complaints into specific, positive, actionable requests:

**Formula**: "When [specific situation], I feel [emotion]. Instead, I would like you to [specific positive behavior]."

**Rules for BCR**:
1. Must be SPECIFIC (not "be more loving" but "say 'I love you' when you leave for work")
2. Must be POSITIVE (what to DO, not what to STOP doing)
3. Must be BEHAVIORAL (observable action, not feeling or attitude)
4. Must be DOABLE (within partner's actual capacity)
5. Must be given in a SET of 3 options (partner chooses which to try first)

**Example**:
"When I share a problem and you immediately suggest solutions, I feel unheard. Instead, I would like you to:
1. Say 'That sounds really hard' before offering solutions
2. Ask me 'Do you want advice or just to vent?' first
3. Put your hand on mine while I'm talking"

## Re-Romanticizing: Intentional Positive Flooding

**Caring Behaviors List**:
Each partner lists 20+ small, specific things their partner could do that would make them feel loved:
- "Bring me coffee in the morning"
- "Text me something sweet during the day"
- "Ask me about my day and really listen"
- "Hold my hand in public"
- "Leave a note in my lunchbox"

Each partner does at least 3 items from their partner's list daily - without being asked.

**Surprise List**:
Each partner lists activities they'd love to do together. Exchange lists. Surprise each other with planned dates from the list.

**The Zero Negativity Challenge**:
For a defined period (start with 1 day), commit to ZERO negative comments. No criticism, sarcasm, eye-rolling, or complaints. Only positive or neutral communication.

## The Container Exercise

For processing deep attachment injuries:

1. **Appointment**: Set a specific time (not during conflict)
2. **Container**: Receiver commits to hold the space without defending
3. **Sender speaks**: Uses Imago Dialogue format to share the wound
4. **Receiver mirrors, validates, empathizes**: Fully receives without counter-arguments
5. **Sender closes**: "Thank you for hearing me"
6. **Receiver responds**: "Thank you for sharing that with me"
7. **Switch**: If needed, schedule a separate session for the other partner

**Rule**: The Container is NOT a debate. There is no "your turn to rebut." Each partner's experience is valid as THEIR experience.
